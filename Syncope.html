Syncope (Fainting)

​

​Immediate Care and First Aid Steps

Overview

​

​Syncope is a sudden, brief loss of consciousness caused by a temporary drop in blood flow to the brain. While most fainting episodes are brief and not life-threatening, they can sometimes signal a serious underlying medical condition or lead to secondary injuries from falling.

​When to Seek Emergency Care

​Call 911 (or your local emergency number) immediately if the person:

  • ​Does not regain consciousness within one minute.
  • ​Is pregnant or has a known heart condition.
  • Experiences chest pain, palpitations, or shortness of breath.
  • ​Suffered a head injury during the fall.
  • ​Shows signs of a seizure (e.g., jerking movements, loss of bladder control, or tongue biting).
  • ​Has difficulty speaking, blurred vision, or numbness in limbs (signs of a stroke).
  • ​Fainted while exercising or after a sudden startle.

​First Aid Treatment

​If you see someone feeling faint or if they have already collapsed, follow these steps:

​1. Position the Person Safely

  • ​If they haven't fallen yet, help them lie down on their back to prevent injury.
  • ​If they are already on the ground, leave them there but ensure the area is safe.

​2. Improve Blood Flow (The Elevation Rule)

  • ​Lift the legs:If there are no suspected injuries to the head, neck, or back, raise the person's feet about 12 inches (30 cm) above heart level. This helps blood return to the brain
  • ​Loosen clothing: Undo belts, collars, or any tight garments that might restrict breathing or circulation.

​3. Check Airway and Breathing

  • ​Ensure the person is breathing clearly.
  • ​If the person does not wake up quickly, turn them onto their side (Recovery Position) to prevent them from choking if they vomit

​4. Monitor Recovery

  • ​Once they wake up, keep them lying down for at least 10–15 minutes.
  • ​Do not let them get up too quickly; have them sit up first for a few minutes before standing.

​What to Avoid (Precautions)

  • ❌ Do not slap or shake them:Physical force is unnecessary and can cause further injury.
  • ​❌ Do not splash water on their face:This can cause the person to inhale water into their lungs.
  • ​❌ Do not put a pillow under their head: This can curve the neck and potentially block the airway.
  • ​❌ Do not give food or drink: Wait until the person is fully alert and oriented before offering anything to swallow.

​Prevention Tips

  • ​Recognize Warning Signs:If you feel dizzy, nauseated, or get "tunnel vision," lie down immediately.
  • ​Hydration: Drink plenty of fluids, especially during hot weather or physical exertion.
  • ​Avoid Sudden Transitions: Move slowly when shifting from a lying position to a standing one (especially for the elderly).
  • ​Medical Check-up:If fainting episodes recur, consult a doctor to check blood pressure, heart rhythm, and glucose levels.
​⚠️ Note: Even if the person feels "fine" after waking up, it is important to identify the trigger (e.g., dehydration, low blood sugar, or heat) to prevent it from happening again.
