Cardiac Arrest

Immediate Care and First Aid Steps

Overview

​

Cardiac arrest is a sudden loss of heart function, breathing, and consciousness. It usually results from an electrical disturbance in the heart that disrupts its pumping action, stopping blood flow to the body. Unlike a heart attack (which is caused by blocked blood flow), cardiac arrest is primarily an electrical problem. Without immediate treatment, cardiac arrest can be fatal within minutes.

When to Seek Emergency Care

Call 911 (or your local emergency number) immediately if someone:

  • Suddenly collapses and becomes unresponsive.
  • Has no pulse or heartbeat.
  • Stops breathing or only gasps irregularly.
  • Loses consciousness without warning

Treatment

Major Cardiac Arrest (Emergency Response)

While waiting for emergency medical teams, provide the following first aid:

  • Check responsiveness:Tap the person and shout to see if they respond.
  • Call for help: Dial emergency services and, if available, ask someone to bring an AED (Automated External Defibrillator).
  • Check breathing and pulse:If absent, begin CPR immediately.
  • Start CPR: Push hard and fast in the center of the chest (about 2 inches deep, 100–120 compressions per minute).
  • Use AED:As soon as it arrives, turn it on, follow the voice prompts, and deliver a shock if advised.
  • Continue CPR: Alternate chest compressions and rescue breaths (if trained), until medical help arrives or the person shows signs of life.

Minor Cardiac Events (Warning Signs)

Not all heart problems lead directly to cardiac arrest. Seek medical evaluation if someone experiences

  • Chest pain or pressure.
  • Shortness of breath.
  • Dizziness or fainting spells.
  • Palpitations or irregular heartbeat.
  • Unexplained fatigue.
Early detection and treatment of these symptoms can prevent cardiac arrest.

What to Avoid (Precautions)

  • ❌ Do not delay calling emergency services.
  • ❌ Do not stop CPR unless the person regains consciousness or professional help takes over.
  • ❌ Do not ignore warning signs like fainting or chest pain.

    • When to Contact a Doctor

    • If you have a family history of sudden cardiac arrest or heart disease.
    • If you experience unexplained fainting, chest pain, or irregular heartbeat.
    • After recovery from cardiac arrest, ongoing medical care and lifestyle changes are essential to prevent recurrence.
